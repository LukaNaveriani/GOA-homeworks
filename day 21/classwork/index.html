Penguin

Penguins are flightless birds that are highly adapted for swimming. They are found in the Southern Hemisphere, particularly in Antarctica. Penguins have unique tuxedo-like black and white feathers and are known for their playful behavior and social nature. They spend most of their time in the water hunting for fish and other small marine creatures. Despite their inability to fly, penguins are excellent swimmers, using their wings as flippers to propel themselves through the water.
